# Proposal — Intake & Document-Chasing Automation for Elder Law Firm A (real firm, Union County NJ)

## What we heard
Every new matter starts with someone "re-typing the same client details into three places," and your team chases clients for missing documents by phone — "sometimes for weeks," with nobody owning the follow-up. You've also been clear on what you *don't* want: "another portal our clients won't log into." The goal is to get the document-chasing problem gone before the fall rush.

## Proposed roadmap

**Phase 1 (core) — target ~8 weeks, before the fall rush**

- **One intake, entered once.** A single intake step (replacing the paper forms and the shared-inbox contact form) captures client details one time and hands them off cleanly — so nobody re-types the same information into three places to open a matter.
- **Document chasing that owns itself.** Automatic follow-up reminders go out over email (Outlook, no new portal for clients to log into) until each requested document is in. Every request has a clear owner and status, so "nobody owns it" stops being true and the weeks-long phone chases end.
- **Reminders that sound like your firm, not a robot.** You review and approve the wording before anything sends. The messages read like they came from your office, and you keep the legacy practice management software exactly as-is — this wraps around it, it doesn't replace it.

**Phase 2 (optional, discuss after Phase 1 is running)**
Once intake and document-chasing are working, a natural next step is extending the same reminder approach to other routine client touchpoints. Entirely optional — only if you want it after you've seen Phase 1 in production.
